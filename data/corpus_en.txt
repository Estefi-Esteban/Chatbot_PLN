Artificial intelligence or AI is the capability of computational systems to perform tasks associated with human intelligence.
AI includes tasks such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and decision-making.
AI is a field of computer science that develops methods enabling machines to perceive their environment.
High-profile applications of AI include advanced web search engines like Google Search.
Recommendation systems used by YouTube, Amazon, and Netflix are examples of AI.
Virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa are common AI applications.
Generative tools like ChatGPT and AI art are part of the modern AI boom.
Artificial intelligence was founded as an academic discipline in 1956.
The field of AI went through cycles of optimism and disappointment known as AI winters.
Funding for AI increased after 2012 when GPUs started accelerating neural networks.
Deep learning outperformed previous AI techniques leading to rapid progress.
Natural language processing or NLP has its roots in the 1950s.
Alan Turing published Computing Machinery and Intelligence in 1950 proposing the Turing test.
The Turing test is a criterion of intelligence involving the interpretation of natural language.
The Georgetown experiment in 1954 involved automatic translation of Russian sentences into English.
ELIZA was a simulation of a Rogerian psychotherapist developed in the 1960s.
Symbolic NLP was the dominant paradigm from the 1950s until the early 1990s.
Statistical NLP emerged in the 1990s with the introduction of machine learning algorithms.
Google BERT and OpenAI GPT-3 are examples of large language models.
Ethical concerns have been raised about AI long-term effects and potential risks.
